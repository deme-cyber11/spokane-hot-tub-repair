How to Choose a Hot Tub Repair Contractor in Spokane

What to look for before you hire anyone to work on your spa.

March 2026 4 min read

When your hot tub breaks down, you want it fixed quickly — but hiring the wrong contractor can lead to shoddy work, inflated bills, or damage that makes the original problem worse. Spokane has several options for hot tub repair, so here is how to tell a reliable professional from the rest.

1. Verify Licensing and Insurance

This is non-negotiable. Any contractor working on your hot tub should hold a valid Washington state contractor license and carry both general liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage. Hot tub repair involves electrical work, plumbing, and heavy components — if an unlicensed or uninsured worker gets hurt on your property or damages your spa, you could be on the hook. Always ask for proof before work begins.

2. Look for Specialized Experience

General handymen and pool companies can sometimes handle basic hot tub issues, but hot tubs are specialized equipment with unique electrical systems, compact plumbing layouts, and brand-specific control boards. Look for a contractor with dedicated hot tub and spa repair experience — not someone who does it as a side service. Ask how long they have been repairing hot tubs specifically and what brands they are most familiar with.

3. Check Reviews and Reputation

Online reviews tell you a lot about a company's reliability. Look at Google reviews, Yelp, and the BBB for consistent patterns. A few things to watch for:

  • Volume matters — A company with 100+ reviews and a 4.8-star average is more trustworthy than one with 5 perfect reviews.
  • Read the negatives — Every company gets an occasional bad review. What matters is how they respond and whether the complaints reveal a pattern (missed appointments, surprise charges, recurring issues after repair).
  • Ask for references — A reputable company should be happy to connect you with recent customers in the Spokane area.

4. Demand Pricing Transparency

Before any work starts, you should receive a clear, written estimate that breaks down the cost of parts and labor. Be cautious of contractors who:

  • Refuse to give an estimate before starting work
  • Quote a vague range like “somewhere between $200 and $1,000”
  • Charge a high diagnostic fee that is not applied toward the repair
  • Add unexpected charges after the work is done

A trustworthy contractor diagnoses the problem, tells you exactly what it will cost, and gets your approval before proceeding.

5. Ask About Warranty Coverage

Quality repair work should come with a warranty — both on the parts used and the labor performed. Ask specifically:

  • How long is the warranty on parts?
  • How long is the warranty on labor?
  • Do they use OEM (original equipment manufacturer) parts or aftermarket alternatives?
  • What happens if the same issue returns within the warranty period?

A contractor who stands behind their work will have clear answers to all of these questions.

6. Evaluate Response Time

In Spokane's climate, a broken hot tub in January is not something you can put off for two weeks. A good contractor should be able to schedule a service visit within 24 to 48 hours for most issues, with same-day emergency service available for critical failures. If a company cannot get to you within a reasonable timeframe, they may be understaffed or overcommitted.

7. Confirm They Service All Brands

Hot tubs come from dozens of manufacturers, each with their own parts, control systems, and design quirks. Make sure the contractor you hire has experience with your specific brand — or at minimum services all major brands including Jacuzzi, Hot Spring, Sundance, Caldera, Bullfrog, Master Spa, and Marquis. A contractor who specializes in only one brand may not have the parts or knowledge for yours.
