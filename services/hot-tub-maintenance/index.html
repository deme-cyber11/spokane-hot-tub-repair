Hot Tub Maintenance in Spokane, WA

Preventive maintenance plans that keep your hot tub running efficiently and prevent costly repairs.

Keep Your Hot Tub Running Like New

Regular maintenance is the single best way to extend the life of your hot tub and avoid expensive emergency repairs. Spokane's climate — with freezing winters that can drop well below zero and warm summers that push your spa's cooling systems — puts extra stress on every component. At Inland NW Hot Tubs, we offer comprehensive maintenance services designed specifically for the demands of the Inland Northwest.

Our licensed and insured technicians perform thorough inspections, cleanings, and adjustments that keep your water crystal clear, your equipment running efficiently, and your energy bills under control. We serve all of Spokane County with free estimates and flexible scheduling.

What Our Maintenance Service Includes

Every maintenance visit is a complete checkup of your hot tub's critical systems. Here's what we cover:

  • Water testing and chemical balancing — We test pH, alkalinity, calcium hardness, and sanitizer levels, then adjust everything to the ideal range for safe, comfortable soaking and equipment longevity.
  • Filter cleaning and inspection — Dirty or worn filters are one of the top causes of poor water quality and pump strain. We deep-clean your filters and let you know when it's time for a replacement.
  • Jet and plumbing inspection — We check for proper jet operation, look for early signs of leaks, and flush the plumbing lines to remove biofilm buildup.
  • Heater and pump checkup — We inspect heating elements, thermostat function, pump seals, and motor condition to catch small problems before they become big ones.
  • Cover inspection — We check your cover for waterlogging, tears, and seal integrity. A damaged cover can dramatically increase your heating costs, especially during Spokane winters.
  • Electrical safety check — We verify GFCI operation, inspect wiring connections, and test the control panel for any error codes or signs of wear.

Seasonal Maintenance for Spokane Winters

Winterization is critical for hot tub owners in the Spokane area. If you plan to shut your hot tub down for the winter, improper draining can leave water trapped in plumbing lines that freezes and cracks fittings, pumps, or even the heater manifold. We offer full winterization services that protect your equipment through the coldest months.

For those who use their hot tub year-round — and with Spokane winters, a hot soak under the snow is hard to beat — our seasonal tune-ups ensure your heater is operating at peak efficiency and your cover is sealing properly to keep heat in and energy costs down.

Why Preventive Maintenance Saves You Money

A single pump failure or heater replacement can cost $300 to $800 or more. Regular maintenance catches worn seals, corroded connections, and chemical imbalances before they cause component failures. Most of our maintenance customers go years without needing a major repair, and their equipment lasts significantly longer than neglected units.

What to Expect During a Professional Hot Tub Maintenance Visit

Many hot tub owners in Spokane maintain their water chemistry diligently but skip the mechanical and electrical checks that keep a spa running safely and efficiently for years. A professional maintenance visit from Inland NW Hot Tubs goes beyond what any homeowner can do from the edge of the deck.

During a standard maintenance visit, we inspect and test the heating element for reduced output or early failure signs, check all pump seals and O-rings for wear, verify that the GFCI protection is functioning correctly, clean or assess the condition of the filters, test water chemistry and make adjustments, lubricate all moving components, and check the cabinet and cover for moisture intrusion or structural issues.

Catching a worn pump seal during a scheduled maintenance call costs a fraction of what it costs after the seal fails and water damages the motor. The same is true for a heating element that's running at reduced efficiency — replacing it proactively is far less disruptive than an emergency repair in January when your spa goes cold.

We recommend at least one professional service visit per year for all Spokane-area hot tub owners, and twice per year for tubs used heavily year-round. Call (509) 236-7423 to schedule your maintenance appointment.
